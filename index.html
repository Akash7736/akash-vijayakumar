Autonomous Surface Vehicle (ASV)

This ASV was designed for environmental monitoring in shallow water environments. It features a catamaran hull design for stability and multiple sensor mounting points.

  • Dimensions: 1.5m × 0.8m × 0.4m
  • Weight: 35kg
  • Max Speed: 5 knots
  • Battery Life: 8 hours

Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV)

This AUV was developed for deep-sea exploration and data collection. Its streamlined design allows for efficient movement underwater while housing multiple scientific instruments.

  • Dimensions: 2.1m × 0.5m × 0.5m
  • Weight: 120kg
  • Max Depth: 500m
  • Battery Life: 12 hours

About Me

Laboratory Researcher & Developer

I’m Akash Vijayakumar, a robotics engineer specializing in marine autonomy. With over three years of experience working on autonomous surface vessels (ASVs) and autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs), my focus lies in developing cutting-edge navigation, perception, and control algorithms for real-world maritime applications.

I hold a B.Tech in Naval Architecture and Ocean Engineering from IIT Madras, where I’ve worked extensively on autonomous docking, trajectory prediction, and SLAM-based costmap learning. My research has been presented at IEEE and OMAE conferences, and I’ve competed in international robotics challenges, including Njord Autonomous Ship Challenge and Virtual RobotX, securing top positions.

Currently, I’m working on vision-based docking algorithms and Gaussian splatting-based SLAM for AUVs. Beyond research, I actively mentor students in marine robotics and aim to bridge the gap between academia and industry by developing autonomy solutions for next-generation marine systems.

Feel free to explore my projects and reach out if you're interested in collaboration!
